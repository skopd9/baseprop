# UK Landlord Compliance Guide

## Overview

As a landlord in the United Kingdom, you must comply with various legal requirements to rent out your property safely and legally. This guide covers all essential compliance requirements for UK residential landlords.

## üî¥ Mandatory Certificates & Checks

### 1. Gas Safety Certificate
**Required:** Annually  
**Applies to:** All properties with gas appliances  
**Who can do it:** Gas Safe registered engineer

- Must be completed annually
- Covers all gas appliances, fittings, and flues
- Copy must be provided to tenants within 28 days of check
- Keep records for 2 years
- **Penalties:** Up to ¬£6,000 fine and/or 6 months imprisonment

**Resources:**
- Find a Gas Safe engineer: https://www.gassaferegister.co.uk/
- HSE guidance: https://www.hse.gov.uk/gas/domestic/faqlandlord.htm

### 2. EICR (Electrical Installation Condition Report)
**Required:** Every 5 years  
**Applies to:** All properties  
**Who can do it:** Qualified and competent electrician

- Required for all private rented properties in England since June 2020
- Inspection of fixed electrical installations
- Copy must be provided to new tenants before occupation
- Existing tenants must receive copy within 28 days of inspection
- **Penalties:** Fines up to ¬£30,000

**Resources:**
- Government guidance: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/electrical-safety-standards-in-the-private-rented-sector-guidance-for-landlords-tenants-and-local-authorities

### 3. Energy Performance Certificate (EPC)
**Required:** Every 10 years  
**Applies to:** All properties  
**Valid for:** 10 years  
**Minimum rating:** E or above

- Must be obtained before marketing a property for rent
- Minimum rating of E required (exceptions apply for some period properties)
- Free to view on government register
- **Penalties:** Up to ¬£5,000 for non-compliance

**Resources:**
- Check your EPC: https://www.gov.uk/find-energy-certificate
- Get an EPC: https://www.gov.uk/get-new-energy-certificate

### 4. Deposit Protection
**Required:** Within 30 days of receiving deposit  
**Applies to:** All assured shorthold tenancies  
**Maximum deposit:** 5 weeks' rent (rent under ¬£50,000/year)

Use one of these government-approved schemes:
- MyDeposits: https://www.mydeposits.co.uk/
- Deposit Protection Service (DPS): https://www.depositprotection.com/
- Tenancy Deposit Scheme (TDS): https://www.tenancydepositscheme.com/

You must:
- Protect deposit within 30 days
- Provide tenant with prescribed information
- **Penalties:** Court can order repayment of 1-3x deposit amount

### 5. Right to Rent Checks
**Required:** Before tenancy starts  
**Applies to:** All tenants in England  
**Who:** Landlord or letting agent

- Check every adult (18+) who will live in property
- Use original documents (passport, visa, etc.)
- Keep copies for duration of tenancy + 1 year
- Repeat checks for those with time-limited immigration status
- **Penalties:** Up to ¬£3,000 per illegal occupant; criminal prosecution possible

**Resources:**
- Right to Rent guide: https://www.gov.uk/check-tenant-right-to-rent-documents
- Landlord checking service: https://www.gov.uk/employee-immigration-employment-status

### 6. Legionella Risk Assessment
**Required:** Before each new tenancy  
**Applies to:** All properties  

- Assess risk of exposure to legionella bacteria
- Focus on water systems
- Simple properties can self-assess
- Implement control measures
- Review assessment regularly

**Resources:**
- HSE guidance: https://www.hse.gov.uk/legionnaires/landlords.htm
- Download free template: https://www.hse.gov.uk/pubns/indg458.htm

### 7. Smoke Alarms
**Required:** At all times  
**Applies to:** All properties  

- At least one alarm on every storey used as living accommodation
- Must be tested at start of each tenancy
- **Penalties:** Up to ¬£5,000 fine

### 8. Carbon Monoxide Alarms
**Required:** At all times  
**Applies to:** Rooms with solid fuel appliances  

- Required in any room with solid fuel burning appliance (coal, wood)
- Must be tested at start of each tenancy
- **Penalties:** Up to ¬£5,000 fine

**Resources:**
- Government guidance: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/smoke-and-carbon-monoxide-alarms-explanatory-booklet-for-landlords

## üè† HMO-Specific Requirements

If your property is a House in Multiple Occupation (HMO), additional requirements apply:

### HMO License
**Required:** If property meets HMO definition  
**Valid for:** Up to 5 years  

An HMO is usually a property where:
- 3 or more tenants live there, forming more than 1 household
- Toilet, bathroom, or kitchen facilities are shared

**Mandatory licensing applies if:**
- 5+ people from 2+ households share facilities
- Building is 3+ storeys

Many local councils have additional or selective licensing schemes.

**How to apply:**
- Contact your local council
- Fee varies by council (typically ¬£500-¬£1,000)
- Must meet management standards
- Property must meet safety standards

**Penalties:** Unlimited fine and/or rent repayment order

### Additional HMO Requirements

1. **Fire Safety Certificate**
   - Fire doors with self-closing devices
   - Fire detection and alarm system
   - Emergency escape lighting
   - Fire blankets and extinguishers
   - Annual inspection recommended

2. **Enhanced Safety Features**
   - Larger kitchen facilities
   - More bathrooms/toilets
   - Minimum room sizes
   - Fire escape routes

**Resources:**
- HMO licensing: https://www.gov.uk/private-renting/houses-in-multiple-occupation
- Fire safety in HMOs: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/fire-safety-in-specialised-housing

## üìã Additional Landlord Obligations

### How to Rent Guide
- Provide tenants with latest government "How to Rent" guide
- Required for all assured shorthold tenancies in England
- Download: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/how-to-rent

### Tenant Fees Act 2019
- Maximum 5 weeks' rent deposit (rent under ¬£50,000/year)
- Maximum 6 weeks' rent deposit (rent ¬£50,000+/year)
- Can charge for:
  - Holding deposit (max 1 week's rent)
  - Default fees (late rent, lost keys - must be reasonable)
  - Early termination (if in contract)

### Property Standards
- Property must be:
  - Safe and free from hazards
  - Free from damp and mould
  - Have adequate heating and hot water
  - Be weatherproof
  - Have working bathroom and kitchen facilities

### Repairs
- Maintain structure and exterior
- Maintain heating and hot water systems
- Maintain sanitation and water supply
- Usually respond within:
  - Emergency repairs: 24 hours
  - Urgent repairs: 7 days
  - Non-urgent repairs: 28 days

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not protecting deposits within 30 days** - Very costly mistake
2. **Forgetting to provide tenant with deposit scheme information**
3. **Not renewing Gas Safety before expiry** - Leave no gaps
4. **Not checking Right to Rent** - Large fines possible
5. **Taking more than 5 weeks' rent as deposit** - Against the law
6. **Not providing How to Rent guide** - Affects Section 21 validity
7. **Letting EPC rating fall below E** - Can't legally rent it out
